# Operational Cost Reduction Initiative

Presenter: Sarah Johnson
Date: March 15, 2024
Report Type: complex

## Executive Summary
- Monthly operational costs exceeded budget by 25%, impacting profitability
- Root cause analysis revealed inefficient resource utilization and redundant services
- Three solution options developed with varying cost/benefit profiles
- Recommended: Service audit and optimization initiative
- Expected savings: $200K/month with 3-month payback period

## Situation
- Monthly operational budget: $1.2M
- Actual monthly spend: $1.5M (25% over)
- Annualized overspend: $3.6M
- Service subscriptions: 47 active contracts
- Cloud infrastructure utilization: 68% average
- Dedicated team members: 12 FTE across various functions
- Last review of operational efficiency: 6 months ago

## Complication
- Q1 financial targets at risk due to overspend
- Limited available budget for new initiatives
- Pressure to maintain service levels while reducing costs
- Stakeholder concern about long-term sustainability
- Manual processes causing inefficiencies and errors
- Vendor contracts nearing renewal without review

## Root Cause Analysis
- Primary cause: Lack of centralized procurement strategy
- Contributing factor 1: Siloed department spending without oversight
- Contributing factor 2: Legacy systems with high maintenance costs
- Contributing factor 3: Unused or underutilized cloud resources
- Contributing factor 4: No regular review of third-party service contracts
- Contributing factor 5: Manual data entry and reconciliation processes

## Key Question
What approach should we take to reduce operational costs while maintaining service quality and supporting growth objectives?

## Solution Options

### Option 1: Service Audit and Optimization
Description: Comprehensive review of all services with cost-benefit analysis and optimization
Pros:
- Immediate cost reduction ($200K/month)
- Sustainable long-term savings
- Identifies wasted resources
- Requires cross-departmental collaboration
Cons:
- Implementation complexity
- Short-term resource commitment
- Potential disruption during audit
Cost: $50K for external audit tools
Timeline: 2 months
Expected savings: $200K/month (3-month payback)

### Option 2: Freeze All New Spending
Description: Immediate freeze on all non-essential spending and vendor contracts
Pros:
- Quick implementation (immediate)
- Low cost to implement
- Stops further bleeding
Cons:
- May impact growth initiatives
- Not sustainable long-term solution
- Vendor relationship management issues
- May require future catch-up spending
Cost: $10K internal management
Timeline: Immediate
Expected savings: $150K/month (no payback period)

### Option 3: Contract Renegotiation
Description: Renegotiate all vendor contracts for better terms and volume discounts
Pros:
- Potential for long-term better terms
- Maintains service levels
- Strong vendor relationships through negotiations
Cons:
- Time-intensive process
- Uncertain outcome on savings
- Legal review required
- May not address root cause
Cost: $75K legal + consulting fees
Timeline: 6 months
Expected savings: $180K/month (4-month payback)

## Recommendation
Recommended: Option 1 - Service Audit and Optimization Initiative
Justification:
- Highest monthly savings ($200K)
- Addresses root cause for sustainable improvement
- Payback period of 3 months
- Identifies future optimization opportunities
- Creates foundation for ongoing cost management

Risk Mitigation:
- Phase implementation to minimize disruption
- Maintain clear communication with stakeholders
- Develop rollback plans for critical services
- Schedule optimization during normal business cycles
